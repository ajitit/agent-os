## 4.1 — Objective

Fix postgres container being marked `unhealthy` before `initdb` initialization completes on first run, causing dependent services to fail to start.

---

## 4.2 — Root Cause

The postgres health check had no `retries` (Docker default: 3) and no `start_period` (default: 0s). First-time database initialization (`initdb`) takes ~32 seconds (visible in logs: 18:48:08 → 18:48:40). With 3 retries × 10s interval, Docker exhausts all retries during init and marks the container `unhealthy` before PostgreSQL is actually ready. The backend's `depends_on: condition: service_healthy` then refuses to start.

---

## 4.3 — Fix

```yaml
# Before
healthcheck:
  test: ["CMD-SHELL", "pg_isready -U agentos"]
  interval: 10s
  timeout: 5s

# After
healthcheck:
  test: ["CMD-SHELL", "pg_isready -U agentos"]
  interval: 10s
  timeout: 5s
  retries: 5
  start_period: 30s
```

`start_period: 30s` — Docker does not count health check failures during this grace window. Covers the full initdb cycle. After the grace period, 5 retries × 10s = 50 seconds of further tolerance.

---

## 4.4 — Risk Check

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Masking a real postgres failure | `start_period` only defers counting failures; if postgres genuinely fails after 30s it will still be marked unhealthy |
| No effect on already-initialized volumes | `start_period` still applies but postgres starts in ~2s; health check passes immediately after first check |
